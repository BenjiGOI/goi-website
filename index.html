The Challenge We Address

A critical shortage of eye health workers leaves billions without the care they need.

Good vision is the cornerstone of education and quality of life. Yet, almost 2.2 billion people worldwide have a vision impairment, a reality driven by a critical shortage of trained eye health workers. We believe unconventional, technology-driven strategies are essential to overcome these barriers and prevent avoidable blindness.

Our Story

The Global Ophthalmic Institute was born out of the challenges observed during the COVID-19 pandemic. It became clear that there was a critical lack of high-quality, comprehensive educational resources accessible to eye care professionals globally. Understanding that in-person support isn't always possible, we are leveraging technology and online resources to bridge this gap and support our colleagues around the world.

Our Mission

To facilitate access for the global eye health workforce to quality eye health education resources, thereby strengthening professional training and improving the provision and standards of patient care.

Our Model: The 'How'

Accessible Online Resource

We are developing online resources to ensure high-quality educational content anywhere in the world.

Global Academic Network

We build a network of partner academic institutions and expert teachers from around the globe to source and share world-class educational content.

Practical Learning Tools

Beyond lectures, we develop and source supportive practical guides, workshops, and innovative tools to enhance the hands-on learning experience for students.
